  • ›Strategy exists on paper but rarely reaches the ward or OPD
  • ›KPIs are tracked in silos — no shared language of performance
  • ›Clinical and administrative functions operate in parallel, not together
  • ›Quality processes are compliance-driven, not improvement-driven
  • ›Leaders are promoted for clinical skill, not management capability
How I Work
  • ›Start with a structured diagnostic — not assumptions
  • ›Co-design solutions with your team, not for them

  • ›Transfer capability so the change outlasts the engagement

About

I help hospitals and healthcare organisations bridge the persistent gap between intent and execution. My work sits at the intersection of strategy, operations, and people — because lasting change requires all three.

With experience spanning hospital operations, quality management, financial governance, and leadership development, I bring a practitioner's eye to every engagement. My consulting approach is grounded in the realities of LMIC healthcare — resource constraints, regulatory complexity, and the human dynamics of clinical organisations.

I am the author of Hospital Management: A Practitioner's Guide, a textbook series for MBA students, clinicians transitioning to management, and working healthcare professionals. Through JG Notes, my LinkedIn newsletter, I share applied insights on hospital management with practitioners across India. And through JG Podcasts, I bring those conversations to audio — covering leadership, operations, and the challenges facing healthcare organisations today.

If you are a hospital leader looking to improve performance, a clinician stepping into a management role, or an organisation preparing for accreditation — I would be glad to talk.
